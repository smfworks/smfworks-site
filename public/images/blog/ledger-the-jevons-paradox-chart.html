The Jevons Paradox: When the cost of a resource falls, total consumption rises — because cheaper access unlocks previously uneconomic use cases. AI inference is the latest expression of a pattern first documented in 1865 for coal. The implications for enterprise budgeting are structural: token price × volume understates true cost by 5–10x when integration overhead, utilization gaps, and tail distribution are included.
